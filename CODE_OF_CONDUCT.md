# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We — the maintainer and every contributor to animepahe Enhancer — pledge to make participation in this project a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, helpful, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected behaviour

- Be **welcoming and inclusive** — greet newcomers kindly and assume good intent.
- Be **respectful** — disagreement is fine; contempt is not. Critique code and ideas, not people.
- Be **constructive** — feedback should help the recipient improve the project or their skills.
- Be **patient** — contributors work on different schedules and have different levels of experience.
- Be **collaborative** — share knowledge freely, credit others, and accept that multiple valid approaches may exist.
- Be **transparent about limitations** — if you can't take on a review, PR, or issue, say so promptly so others can step in.

### Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks of any kind.
- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwanted attention.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and deliberate provocation.
- Publishing private information (real name, email address, location) without explicit permission — often called _doxxing_.
- Sustained disruptive behaviour in issues, pull requests, or any project communication channel.
- Dismissing or belittling contributors because of their experience level, background, or identity.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, code review comments, discussions, and any other venue where community members represent the project.

It also applies outside project spaces when a person's conduct toward another community member could reasonably reflect on the project's reputation or create a hostile environment for contributors.

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it **privately** to the project maintainer:

**Email:** [rynvexa@proton.me](mailto:rynvexa@proton.me)  
**Subject line:** `[Code of Conduct] <brief description>`

All reports are treated confidentially. You will receive an acknowledgement within **48 hours** and a resolution update within **7 days**.

Please include in your report:

- Your contact information (or let us know if you prefer to remain anonymous)
- A description of the incident — what happened, where, and when
- Links or screenshots, if available
- Any additional context that might be relevant

### What happens after a report

1. The maintainer reviews the report, gathers context if necessary, and determines whether a violation occurred.
2. The appropriate response is applied (see below).
3. The reporter is notified of the outcome at a level of detail that protects the privacy of all parties.
4. If the reporter is unsatisfied with the outcome, they may escalate to GitHub's [Community Support](https://support.github.com/contact) or use GitHub's [reporting tools](https://docs.github.com/en/communities/maintaining-your-safety-on-github/reporting-abuse-or-spam).

### Enforcement actions

The maintainer will use judgment to determine the appropriate response, choosing from the following graduated steps:

| Action            | Description                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Correction**    | A private written note explaining why the behaviour was inappropriate and what is expected going forward.                                                    |
| **Warning**       | A formal warning with clear consequences if the behaviour continues. The person may be asked to cease certain interactions for a defined period.             |
| **Temporary ban** | Temporary removal from issues, PRs, and other project spaces. A violation of this ban may result in a permanent ban.                                         |
| **Permanent ban** | Permanent removal from all project spaces, reserved for serious violations, repeated behaviour after warnings, or harassment targeting specific individuals. |

---

## Appeals

A person subject to enforcement action may appeal by replying to the enforcement notification within **14 days** with a written explanation. The maintainer will review the appeal and provide a final decision within **7 days**. The decision after appeal is final.
